Recommended Books

You do not need to read all of these. Find the one that speaks to your current challenge and start there.

On Leadership & People

  • Radical Candor — Kim Scott. Giving direct, caring feedback.
  • Turn the Ship Around! — L. David Marquet. Creating leaders at every level.
  • The Manager's Path — Camille Fournier. The IC-to-leader journey for engineers.
  • An Elegant Puzzle — Will Larson. Systems thinking for engineering management.
  • Dare to Lead — Brené Brown. Courage, vulnerability, and empathy.
  • Inside-Out Leadership — Rajiv Vij. Self-awareness as the foundation of leadership.
  • Crucial Conversations — Patterson et al. Navigating high-stakes disagreements.
  • The Five Dysfunctions of a Team — Patrick Lencioni. Why teams fail and how to fix it.
  • Thanks for the Feedback — Stone & Heen. How people process and receive feedback.
  • The Pyramid Principle — Barbara Minto. The definitive guide to structured communication.
  • Executive Presence — Sylvia Ann Hewlett. Breaking down presence into concrete, learnable skills.

On Code Quality & Engineering

  • Clean Code — Robert C. Martin. Writing maintainable code.
  • Effective Java — Joshua Bloch. Best practices for Java.
  • Refactoring — Martin Fowler. Systematically improving code quality.
  • A Philosophy of Software Design — John Ousterhout. Thinking about complexity.
  • Release It! — Michael Nygard. Building production-ready software.

On Delivery & Teams

  • Accelerate — Forsgren, Humble & Kim. The science of high-performing teams.
  • The Phoenix Project — Gene Kim. DevOps, flow, and eliminating waste.
  • Team Topologies — Skelton & Pais. How team structure affects delivery.
