Self-Assessment

An honest checklist to see where you are today. No grades, no scores reported anywhere, no manager watching. Pick this up every few months and watch yourself grow.

Check what’s true for you today — not what you intend to do, not what you did once last year. Your ticks are saved in your browser so you can come back to this page over time. Nothing is sent anywhere.

When you’re done, the summary at the bottom will point you at the sections of the guide most worth your next hour.

Scoring Guide

The assessment has about 72 items. The exact count is shown at the top — it shifts slightly as content evolves. What matters more than the number is which items you ticked and which you left blank.

Top quartile: You’re operating with strong leadership habits across the board. Keep investing in the sections that still feel weakest.

Upper-middle: Solid foundation — pick your weakest section and focus there for 2 weeks. Most leaders live here for years.

Lower-middle: You’re likely still spending most energy on IC work. Re-read Foundations, People & Culture, and work through The 12-Week Plan.

Just starting: Start with one section. Pick 3 items and commit to them this week. Progress, not perfection.

If this guide feels like a stretch in every section: you may be stepping into leadership from a stage where the IC habits aren’t fully set yet. The Craft & Ownership guide (and, one step earlier, Curiosity & Commitment) cover the foundation this one builds on. Strong ICs make stronger leaders — build there first, come back here when you do.

If you lead in a product company rather than consulting, skip the Consulting Mindset section and count the remaining items. The advice remains the same.

The greatest leader is not the one who does the greatest things, but the one who gets people to do the greatest things.

Come back next month. Check more boxes. That’s growth.
